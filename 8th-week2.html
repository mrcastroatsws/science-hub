The Electron Highway

Conductors: Materials that let electrons flow easily. (e.g., Copper, Gold, Water).

Insulators: Materials that STOP electron flow. (e.g., Rubber, Plastic, Glass).

HOOK "Why are wires covered in plastic? Why aren't they just bare metal?"

MMVP Voice: 0. Materials: Notebooks open to definitions page.

Which is it?

ACMO Watch for students guessing. Ask "Why?" for each answer. "Does it have free electrons?"

Activity: The Tester

Task: Build a circuit with a gap.

Procedure: Place different items in the gap. If the bulb lights up, it's a conductor.

PREDICTION -> TEST -> CONCLUSION

MATERIALS FOSS Kit (Motors/Bulbs), random classroom items (keys, chalk, foil).

Lab Results

Surprise Finding: Did the graphite (pencil lead) light the bulb?

Concept: Semi-conductors exist. Not everything is 100% yes or no.

Electrical Safety

Rule 1: Water + Electricity = Danger.

Rule 2: Never touch a bare wire.

Rule 3: Short circuits cause heat/fire.

DISCUSSION ROUTINE "Respect the Speaker." When someone is sharing a safety story, we are at Voice Level 0.
