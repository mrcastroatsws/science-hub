Reviewing CW #1

Question: "Are all Chordates Vertebrates?"

Answer: NO.

Some chordates (like tunicates and lancelets) never develop a backbone.

STAMP Misconception check: A chordate has a nerve cord. A vertebrate puts that nerve cord inside bone.

MMVP Voice: 0. Hand back graded CW#1 papers.

Classifying Life

Cladogram: A diagram showing patterns of shared characteristics.

It links groups of organisms by showing how evolutionary lines branched off from common ancestors.

TEXTBOOK Miller & Levine TE p. 516 (Interpreting Cladograms).

ENGAGE "Which two animals on this chart are most closely related? How do you know?"

Building a Cladogram

Task: Group these animals based on derived characters.

Traits: Lungs, Claws, Fur, Mammary Glands.

Animals: Lizard, Mouse, Salamander, Chimp.

ACMO Walk the room. Ensure students place the "least complex" animal (Salamander) at the start.

MMVP Voice: 2 (Group work). Materials: Notebooks only.

What makes a Primate?

1. Binocular Vision (Depth perception).

2. Long fingers & toes.

3. Arms that rotate around shoulder joints.

4. Large cerebrum (Brain).

TEXTBOOK Miller & Levine TE Chapter 26 (p. 765).

DEMO Have students try to pick up a pencil without using their thumbs. "Opposable thumbs allow precision grip."

Hominine Evolution

Bipedalism: Walking on two feet.

Opposable Thumbs: Grasping tools.

Lucy: Australopithecus afarensis (3.2 million years ago).

ACMO Ensure students understand that humans did not evolve *from* chimps, but share a *common ancestor*.
