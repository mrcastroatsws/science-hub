Functions of Money

  1. Medium of Exchange: Used to buy goods/services.
  2. Unit of Account: A "price tag" to compare values.
  3. Store of Value: Holds its purchasing power for the future.

Types of Money

Commodity

Value in itself (Gold, Cacao).

Representative

Paper backed by gold/silver.

Fiat

Value by Gov decree (Lempira, Dollar).

Characteristics

Portability: Easy to carry.
Divisibility: Can be broken into smaller change.
Uniformity: All $1 bills look the same.

Activity: Ask students why a "Diamond" would be bad money. (Hard to divide without losing value).
