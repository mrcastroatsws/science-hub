The Language of Chemistry

H2O vs. H2O2

Chemical Formula: Indicates the relative number of atoms of each kind in a chemical compound.

Subscript: Small number to the right. Indicates quantity of the atom.

TEACHER VOICE

"One tiny number changes water (life) into hydrogen peroxide (poison). Precision matters."

MON | PRACTICE

Counting Atoms

(NH4)2SO4

Ammonium Sulfate

N = 2
H = 8
S = 1
O = 4
MISCONCEPTION ALERT

Students often forget to distribute the subscript outside the parenthesis. "The 2 belongs to the Nitrogen AND the Hydrogen."
