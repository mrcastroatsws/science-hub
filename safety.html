The "Lab" Reality Check

Southwest School possesses a designated Science Laboratory. However, due to current equipment constraints and supply limitations, full-scale wet labs will be infrequent this year.

Therefore, the Classroom IS the Laboratory.

Safety rules apply whether we are in the Lab room or at our desks handling models. Respect for materials is non-negotiable.

Personal Protective Equipment

When instructed, Goggles and Aprons must be worn. There are no exceptions for "just watching." If the gear is out, it is on.

The "Zero" Zone

No food. No drinks (even water). No gum. Contamination risks are real, even with household chemicals.

Breakage Protocol

If glass breaks or liquid spills: FREEZE. Do not touch it. Raise your hand and call "Code Blue" immediately. I will handle the cleanup.

02. Routines & Expectations

"We are building habits of excellence. When I say these words, this is what they mean."

MMVP

BEHAVIOR
  • Movement: Are we seated or standing?
  • Materials: Are hands empty or writing?
  • Voice: Level 0 (Silent) to 3 (Presenting).
  • Participation: How do you show engagement?

At-Bats

PRACTICE

Just like baseball, you need reps to hit a home run.

When I say "At-Bat," it means independent, silent practice. You are testing your own brain, not your neighbor's.

Turn & Talk

DISCOURSE

Knee-to-Knee.

1. Turn body to partner.
2. Partner A speaks (30s).
3. Partner B listens activey.
4. Switch.

Cold Call

ACCOUNTABILITY

I will call on students randomly, not just those with hands up.

Why? Because every mind matters, and everyone must be ready to contribute.
