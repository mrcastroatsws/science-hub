The Blackout

"Imagine a world completely without electricity."

No internet. No refrigeration. No hospitals.
Electricity isn't magic; it is the flow of electrons through a closed loop.

HOOK Turn off the classroom lights for 5 seconds. Ask: "What just happened to the electrons?"

The Parts of a Circuit

1. Source

Provides the push.
(Battery, Generator)

2. Receiver

Uses the energy.
(Bulb, Motor, Phone)

3. Path

The road for electrons.
(Copper Wire)

Open vs. Closed

Analogy: The Water Pipe

If the pipe is cut, water spills and flow stops.

If the wire is cut (Open), electrons stop.

MISCONCEPTION Students think electricity 'leaks' out of an open wire. Correct them: It simply stops moving.

DANGER ZONE

The Short Circuit

PATH WITH NO RECEIVER = FIRE

Electricity loves the path of least resistance. Without a bulb to slow it down, the wire gets HOT.

True or False?

"A short circuit is safe because it uses less wire."

FALSE
