Short Circuit

Definition: A path with a Source, but NO Receiver.

Result: The energy has nowhere to go but heat.

DANGER: Fire / Burned fingers.

Simulation Case Study

915 mA vs 20 mA

The Scenario: We connected a 9V Battery directly to an LED in Tinkercad.

The Reading: 915 mA (0.915 Amps).

The Problem: An LED can only handle 20 mA.

We are pumping 45x the safety limit into that light.

TEACHER SCRIPT: "If the LED is still lighting up in the simulation, it is in its 'death throes.' It would physically burn out in seconds. Why? Because we opened the floodgates."

Why did this happen?

1. The Floodgate Analogy
The LED is a tiny garden hose valve (2V limit). The Battery is a Fire Hydrant (9V pressure). We connected them directly. The valve bursts.
2. Why stop at 915 mA?
Why didn't it go to 10,000 mA? Because 9V batteries are weak. The battery itself is "choking" (Internal Resistance).
TEACHER NOTE: "Internal Resistance is why the battery gets hot. The battery is struggling to push that much energy out."

The Fix: Resistors

To fix this, we need a Bottleneck.

A resistor slows down the current to safe levels.

[Image of resistor calculation]

R = (9V - 2V) / 0.02A = 350 Ohms

TEACHER SCRIPT: "We will learn Ohm's Law later, but for now, know this: Never connect an LED directly to a battery without a resistor."
