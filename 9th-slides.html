TUE | THE LEAP

The Inference

An inference is a conclusion reached on the basis of evidence and reasoning.

It is your brain trying to "connect the dots."

Observation: "The street is wet."
Inference: "It must have rained."
TUE | CRITICAL THINKING

The Trap

Correlation ≠ Causation

Just because two things happen at the same time (Association), does not mean one caused the other.

DATA POINT A

Ice Cream sales go UP.

DATA POINT B

Drowning deaths go UP.

Bad Inference: "Eating ice cream causes drowning."

TUE | THE SOLUTION

The Confounder

To find Causation, you must rule out other factors.

The Hidden Variable: Summer Heat

Heat causes people to buy Ice Cream.
Heat causes people to go swimming.

SCIENCE FAIR TIP:

This is why you need Control Variables. To prove your variable caused the change, you must "freeze" everything else.
