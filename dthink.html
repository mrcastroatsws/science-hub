MODULE 3.1.1

Empathize

"It's Not About You"

Before you build, you must understand the User.

Application: Who is affected by the problem you are solving? Is it farmers? Doctors? Students?

3.1.1 ACTIVITY

The Interview

You cannot guess what people need. You must ask.

"Tell me about the last time you..."

MODULE 3.1.2

Define

"The Problem Statement"

Data without direction is useless. You must craft a Point of View (POV).

User + Need + Insight

3.1.2 ACTIVITY

Mad Libs Statement

"_________ (The User) needs a way to _________ (The Need) because _________ (Surprising Insight)."

MODULE 3.1.3

Ideate

"Quantity over Quality"

This is the brainstorming phase. No idea is "bad" yet. We want wild solutions.

3.1.3 ACTIVITY

Crazy 8s

Fold a paper into 8 sections. Sketch 8 different solutions in 8 minutes.

STOP JUDGING YOURSELF.

MODULE 3.1.4

Prototype

"Build to Think"

A prototype is not the final product. It is a question made physical.

Working on a Project: Your first experiment is a prototype. It will likely fail. That is good.

3.1.4 ACTIVITY

Cardboard & Tape

Do not code yet. Do not buy expensive sensors yet.

Build the concept using trash. Does it fit in the hand? Does it make sense?

MODULE 3.1.5

Test

"Feedback is a Gift"

Testing is not about selling your idea. It is about finding where it breaks.

Rule: Show, don't tell. Hand the user the prototype and say nothing.

3.1.5 ACTIVITY

The Matrix

What worked?
What failed?
Questions?
New Ideas?
